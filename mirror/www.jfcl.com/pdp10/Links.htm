Visit http://www.36bit.org/ for information about any and all computers with 36 bits, including my second favorite - the Univac 1106, but with a special emphasis on DEC.

At http://www.inwap.com/pdp10/ Joe Smith has a really excellent set of -10 and -20 related links, which I won't even attempt to reproduce here.

The guys at http://pdp10.nocrew.org/ have built a gcc cross compiler for the -10 for XKL.

Tim Shoppa has an amazing number of DEC software distributions, including the 6.03A and 7.01 monitors and CUSPS, at http://pdp-10.trailing-edge.com/ .

John Wilson has some random, unsorted, files at ftp://ftp.dbit.com/pub/pdp10/ including, of all things, a listing of the source for the KS10 microcode.
