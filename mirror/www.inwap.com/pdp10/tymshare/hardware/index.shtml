Tymshare PDP-10 hardware

Hardware

Last systems: shutdown December 1998

2 TOAD-1 systems from XKL
  • 32 megawords of memory (equivalent of 72 megabytes)
  • SCSI disk and tape (1/2-inch 6250 and 4mm DAT)
  • 2 ethernets
  • connected to Tymnet via a Sun workstation (X.25 to TELNET interface)

Retired Hardware

  • On 31-Mar-96 they shut down the 4 KL-1090 systems. Each had
    • KL with model-B backplane, console front-end with DECtapes
    • 4 megawords AMPEX external memory
    • SA-10 channel adaptor for IBM disk and tape
    • Memorex 3652 disk strings (16 logical units per string, 300 megabytes per unit)
    • StorageTek 6250-bpi tape drives
    • E-BUS base (DMA connection to the Tymnet network)
    One of the systems ("F34") went to the West Coast Computer Museum.
  • Several KI-10 systems (used smaller 3330-II disks)
  • Several KS-10 systems (DL11 to a PDP-11 which spoke Tymnet protocol)
  • Several Foonly-F3 systems (DL11 emulated in Foonly microcode)
  • Various Foonly-F4 systems (running TOPS-20 or TENEX instead of TYMCOM-X)

Other stuff

Tymshare had several XDS 940 systems directly connected to Tymnet.
